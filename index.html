Deep Sea Mining

Background Countries Affected Solutions Resources

Background

Deep sea mining refers to extracting minerals from the ocean floor, including rare earth elements, cobalt, and manganese nodules. It's driven by demand for electronics, green energy tech, and industrial applications.

However, it poses serious risks: destruction of unknown ecosystems, long-lasting sediment plumes, and noise pollution that disturbs marine life.

Countries Affected

Top 3 regions heavily involved in or impacted by deep sea mining:

  • Papua New Guinea: Site of the Solwara 1 project. Environmental concerns halted progress.
  • Japan: Conducted experimental deep-sea mining off Okinawa.
  • Cook Islands: Granting exploration licenses to companies, raising environmental debates.

Solutions & Conclusions

Global Actions: The UN and environmental groups have called for a moratorium until impacts are better understood.

Individual Actions: Support legislation, raise awareness, reduce demand for mined tech materials.

Our Ideas: Fund marine restoration, incentivize recycling tech components, invest in alternative energy sources that reduce rare mineral dependency.
