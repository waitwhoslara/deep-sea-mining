What is Deep Sea Mining?

Deep sea mining is the process of retrieving mineral resources from the ocean floor, typically at depths of 1,000 meters or more. It targets resources like manganese nodules, polymetallic sulfides, and cobalt-rich crusts.

Why Is It a Problem?

Deep sea mining disrupts fragile marine ecosystems, potentially causing irreversible damage to species we barely understand. It stirs up sediment plumes and noise that can harm sea life far beyond the mining zone.

What Can Be Done?

Some experts call for a global moratorium on deep sea mining until environmental impacts are better understood. Alternatives include improved recycling, land-based mining reforms, and stricter regulations on deep-sea activity.
